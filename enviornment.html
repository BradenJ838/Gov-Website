Fracking produces fewer greenhouse gas emissions than coal when burned for energy generation while producing no substantial damage to the enviornment or drinking water as found the the EPA

Compared to other forms of transportation like trucks or trains, pipelines have lower energy requirements and emissions per unit of gas transported. In regions with extensive natural gas production, excess gas is often flared at the wellhead due to a lack of infrastructure to capture and transport it. Pipelines provide a means to capture this gas and transport it to market instead of allowing it to be wasted through flaring. This reduces both emissions and wasted energy resources.
