Defunding the police is not effective

Reductions in law enforcement presence and response capabilities leads to increased crime and less effective policing. Many police departments across the country are facing staffing shortages, which can strain resources and compromise public safety. By providing additional funding, agencies can recruit and retain qualified officers, reducing response times and improving coverage in high-crime areas.

Our police need more support now than ever. Increasing police funding in areas such as training, crime prevention and monitoring will effectively stop crime. Investing in specialized training on topics such as de-escalation tactics, cultural sensitivity, and crisis intervention can improve officer effectiveness and minimize conflicts with the community.

The country as a whole spent 2% more on police funding which directly led to a 7% decrease in crime across the nation. Increasing police funding works.
